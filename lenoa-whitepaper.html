Abstract

Lenoa introduces a peer-to-peer Bitcoin-backed lending protocol in which every loan position is split into two independently transferable ERC-721 tokens: a Borrower NFT representing the collateral obligation and a Lender NFT representing the capital right. Lenoa's P2P structure enables direct rate negotiation between counterparties, and the dual-NFT primitive creates a continuous secondary market for live loan positions on both sides of the debt agreement. We describe the dual-NFT architecture, the interest accrual mechanism, the two-tier permissioned liquidation model, and the three-feed oracle aggregator. We argue that tokenising both sides of a debt agreement produces a novel financial primitive — a liquid market for directional credit exposure on Bitcoin.

§ 01

Introduction

Bitcoin holders face a recurring problem: they hold a high-value, appreciating asset but cannot access dollar liquidity without selling it. Existing solutions force a choice between custodial centralised lenders — which carry counterparty risk — or over-engineered pooled DeFi protocols that abstract away the lender entirely.

Lenoa proposes a third path. By building on Mezo Network — a Bitcoin-native EVM-compatible blockchain — Lenoa enables direct peer-to-peer Bitcoin-backed loans denominated in MUSD, Mezo's stablecoin, while preserving the composability and programmability of DeFi.

The key insight is that both sides of a loan are valuable financial instruments. The right to collect repayment is a bond-like asset. The obligation to repay against collateral is a leveraged BTC position. Both should be liquid. Making both sides independently tokenised and tradable creates credit markets that have not previously existed on any chain.

§ 02

Design Context

DeFi lending has evolved through three design generations. Understanding the limitations of each clarifies what Lenoa adds.

Generation 1 — Pooled Overcollateralised Lending

The first generation of DeFi lending introduced algorithmic interest rate pools. Lenders deposit into shared pools and receive fungible interest-bearing tokens in return. Borrowers draw from the pool against overcollateralised positions. Rates are set by utilisation curves rather than negotiation. The lender has no relationship with any specific borrower — their capital is pooled and fungible. Liquidation is permissionless: any address can trigger it for a bonus.

Pooled lending is capital-efficient but removes all lender discretion. The lender cannot choose their counterparty, set their own rate, or control liquidation timing. The protocol intermediates everything.

Generation 2 — NFT Collateral Lending

The second generation extended lending to specific, non-fungible collateral. A borrower offers a specific asset; a lender makes a direct loan offer. This is peer-to-peer, and lenders can set their own terms. However, these protocols tokenise only the lender side of the agreement — the lender receives a receipt representing their loan. The borrower's position remains non-transferable.

Generation 3 — Fixed-Rate Bond Protocols

The third generation introduced fixed-rate lending via principal tokens and yield tokens — time-bounded representations of cash flows. These are composable and tradable, but synthetic: they are created by splitting yield-bearing deposits, not by direct bilateral credit agreements between a specific lender and borrower.

Lenoa's Position

Lenoa takes the bilateral matching of Gen 2 and applies NFT tokenisation to both sides of the agreement. The Borrower NFT is a tradable instrument representing a collateralised position — a primitive that does not exist in any prior generation. This creates secondary markets for borrower exposure, not just lender receipts, and enables a new class of on-chain credit instrument.

§ 03

The Dual NFT Model

The core primitive of Lenoa is the simultaneous minting of two linked ERC-721 tokens upon loan funding. These tokens encode the full state of a bilateral credit agreement on-chain.

BN-047 and LN-047 are permanently paired at creation and cannot be uncoupled. Repayment by the holder of BN-047 is always directed to the current holder of LN-047, regardless of how many times either NFT has changed hands. This invariant is enforced at the contract level.

Transfer Semantics

Both NFTs override the OZ v5 _update() hook. When either NFT changes hands, the hook calls InterestAccrual.currentDebt(), snapshotting the accrued interest into storage before the transfer completes. The new holder inherits the position with the exact debt state at the moment of transfer — no interest is lost or gained by the act of transfer itself.

// OZ v5: _update() replaces _beforeTokenTransfer
function _update(
    address to,
    uint256 tokenId,
    address auth
) internal override returns (address from) {
    from = super._update(to, tokenId, auth);
    bool isTransfer = from != address(0) && to != address(0);
    if (isTransfer) {
        // Snapshot accrued interest before new holder takes over
        _snapshotDebt(tokenId / 2);
    }
}
§ 04

Loan Lifecycle

A Lenoa loan passes through five distinct states. The state is stored in the Borrower NFT and enforced by LoanManager.sol.

STATE 01
REQUESTED

Borrower deposits BTC. Loan request posted publicly. No NFTs minted yet.

STATE 02
FUNDED

Lender sends MUSD. BN and LN minted simultaneously. MUSD to borrower.

STATE 03
ACTIVE

Interest accruing. Both NFTs tradable. LTV tracked continuously.

STATE 04a
REPAID

BN holder repays. MUSD to LN holder. BTC returned. Both burned.

STATE 04b
LIQUIDATED

LTV > 75%. Lender (48hr window) or anyone triggers. BTC seized, debt paid, bounty paid, remainder to borrower. Both burned.

Rate Discovery

Unlike algorithmic protocols, Lenoa rates are set by the borrower as a proposal and accepted (or countered) by a lender. The borrower's request includes a proposed rate. Any lender can fund at that rate. The market determines whether a given rate attracts capital — high-risk requests must offer higher rates.

§ 05

Interest Accrual

Interest accrues continuously on all active loans. The protocol uses a simple interest model for the hackathon release, with compound interest planned for v1.

currentDebt(t) = principal × ( 1 + rate × Δt / 365 days )

Simple interest model. Δt is seconds elapsed since last snapshot. Rate is annual (e.g., 0.065 for 6.5% APR).

Interest is not streamed continuously — it is calculated on-demand. When the contract needs the current debt (for repayment, liquidation check, or NFT transfer), it computes the current value using the formula above against the stored snapshot.

Interest on Transfer

The critical invariant: interest earned before a transfer belongs to the seller. The _update() hook snapshots the accrued interest into the debtAtLastUpdate field and records lastUpdated = block.timestamp. The buyer's position starts from this snapshot — they did not earn interest they did not hold the NFT for.

function currentDebt(uint256 loanId) public view returns (uint256) {
    Loan storage loan = loans[loanId];
    uint256 elapsed  = block.timestamp - loan.lastUpdated;
    uint256 interest = loan.debtAtLastUpdate
        * loan.interestRate
        * elapsed
        / 365 days
        / 1e18;
    return loan.debtAtLastUpdate + interest;
}

v1 will migrate to compound interest using the standard e^(rate × t) continuous compounding formula. The simple model is used for the initial release for auditability — the upgrade path is additive and does not affect NFT structure or pairing invariants.

§ 06

Liquidation Design

Lenoa uses a two-tier liquidation model that prioritises the Lender NFT holder while guaranteeing protocol solvency through a permissionless fallback. This design eliminates the lender-inactivity risk that would otherwise make the protocol unsafe in a rapid market downturn.

Two-Tier Access Rules

// Liquidation access — enforced by LoanManager.sol
if (ltv < 75%):
    → No liquidation permitted

else if (ltv >= 75% AND timeSinceLiquidatable < 48 hours):
    → PRIORITY WINDOW: Lender NFT holder only
    → No MUSD payment required — lender receives BTC directly
    → Lender receives: BTC equivalent of outstanding debt (at oracle price)
    → Borrower receives: surplus BTC above debt amount
    → No bounty deducted — exclusive window is the lender's reward

else if (timeSinceLiquidatable >= 48 hours):
    → OPEN LIQUIDATION: Anyone can liquidate (bots, third parties)
    → Liquidator pays outstanding debt in MUSD → Lender NFT holder receives it
    → From seized BTC collateral:
        → Liquidator receives: btcForDebt + btcForBounty
            → btcForDebt   = debt / btcPrice          (reimbursement for MUSD paid)
            → btcForBounty = (0.8% × principal + 10 MUSD) / btcPrice  (profit)
        → Borrower NFT holder receives: remaining BTC after liquidator paid
    → No DEX sell by the protocol — liquidator acquires MUSD and converts externally

Bounty Structure

The Open Liquidation bounty is designed to incentivise liquidator bots on loans of any size. The variable component (0.8% of principal) scales with loan size. The flat kicker (10 MUSD) ensures small loans are also worth liquidating — preventing dust accumulation.

bounty = (0.008 × principal) + 10 MUSD

Example: 30,000 MUSD loan → bounty = 240 + 10 = 250 MUSD. Paid to the liquidator in BTC from seized collateral (250 ÷ btcPrice). The liquidator is also reimbursed in BTC for the full MUSD debt they paid — so their net cost is zero and they profit the bounty.

In all scenarios, the Lender NFT holder is made whole — they receive their principal plus accrued interest, settled in BTC (Priority Window) or MUSD (Open Liquidation). The protocol never sells collateral on a DEX. The liquidator acquires MUSD externally, pays the lender directly, and is reimbursed from BTC collateral plus a bounty. The borrower always receives any BTC surplus above all obligations.

§ 07

Oracle Architecture

All LTV calculations, liquidation triggers, and loan health metrics depend on a single trusted BTC/USD price. Lenoa aggregates three independent price feeds and returns the median, making single-feed manipulation prohibitively expensive.

Pyth Network Pull Oracle · <1s Latency $94,185 LIVE
Stork Ultra-Low Latency · Publisher Network $94,210 LIVE
DEX TWAP On-Chain · Mezo Swap · 1h Window $94,052 LIVE
Median Price — Used for All Calculations $94,185 
function getMedianPrice() external returns (uint256) {
    uint256 pPyth  = _getPyth();
    uint256 pStork = _getStork();
    uint256 pTwap  = _getTwap();
    uint256 median = _median(pPyth, pStork, pTwap);
    _validateDeviation("Pyth",  pPyth,  median);
    _validateDeviation("Stork", pStork, median);
    _validateDeviation("Twap",  pTwap,  median);
    return median;
}

function _median(uint256 a, uint256 b, uint256 c) internal pure returns (uint256) {
    if (a > b) (a, b) = (b, a);
    if (b > c) (b, c) = (c, b);
    if (a > b) (a, b) = (b, a);
    return b; // middle value
}

§ 08

Smart Contract Architecture

Lenoa is composed of five smart contracts. Each has a single responsibility. LoanManager.sol is the only contract that holds funds; all others are stateless or reference-only.

LoanManager.sol

Central coordinator. Receives BTC collateral, creates and validates loan requests, routes MUSD on funding, processes repayments, and coordinates both liquidation tiers. The only contract with custody of funds.

depositAndRequest() fundLoan() repay() claimPriorityWindow() openLiquidation()
LoanNFT.sol

ERC-721 contract. Mints both Borrower NFT and Lender NFT per loan. Stores full Loan struct on-chain. Implements the OZ v5 _update() hook to snapshot accrued interest on every transfer. Burns both NFTs on loan closure.

mintPair() burnPair() loanById() updateDebt()
OracleAggregator.sol

Fetches BTC/USD from Pyth, Stork, and the on-chain DEX TWAP. Sorts the three values and returns the median. Validates freshness and per-feed deviation bounds. Triggers circuit breaker on >25% single-block drop.

getMedianPrice() peekMedianPrice() resetCircuitBreaker()
InterestAccrual.sol

Stateless library called by LoanManager. Computes current debt for any loan given principal, rate, and elapsed time. Handles debt snapshotting on NFT transfers via the hook in LoanNFT.

currentDebt() updateSnapshot() accruedSince()
Treasury.sol

Accumulates protocol origination fees (5% of each loan principal). Controlled by the protocol owner. Emits fee events for off-chain accounting. Designed for DAO governance transfer in v1.

collectFee() withdraw() setFeeRate()
DexTwap.sol

Reads the Mezo Swap BTC/MUSD AMM pool and computes a 1-hour time-weighted average price from cumulative on-chain accumulators. Fully on-chain — no external publisher required. Manipulation requires sustaining an artificial price across the entire TWAP window, making it independent from publisher-based oracle attacks.

update() getPrice() spotPrice() peekPrice()

All parameters are adjustable by the protocol owner via Ownable2Step. The ownership progression follows three stages:

v0 — Hackathon: Deployer EOA owns all contracts. Simplest possible setup; acceptable because no real user funds are at risk during testing.

v0.5 — Testnet: Ownership transferred to a Gnosis Safe multisig (3-of-5). Parameter changes require multiple independent key-holders to sign. Provides meaningful protection without governance overhead.

v1 — Mainnet: On-chain DAO with timelock. Parameter changes are proposed on-chain, subject to a mandatory 48-hour delay, and executable only after community vote. Users can observe and exit before any change takes effect.

§ 10

Secondary Market

The secondary market for Lenoa NFTs is the protocol's most novel property. Unlike receipt tokens from pooled protocols (which represent a share of a pool, not a specific loan), Lenoa NFTs represent bilateral claims with specific, observable risk parameters.

Pricing a Lender NFT

A rational buyer of a Lender NFT would price it as:

fair_value = principal + accrued_interest + expected_future_interest − default_risk_discount

Where default_risk_discount increases as LTV approaches 75%. All inputs are readable on-chain.

Pricing a Borrower NFT

A Borrower NFT buyer is acquiring a leveraged BTC long position with capped downside (liquidation) and full upside. Pricing reflects:

fair_value = collateral_value − outstanding_debt − risk_premium

Distressed positions (LTV >65%) will trade at a discount to intrinsic value. The buyer is pricing in the probability of liquidation before repayment.

With the two-tier liquidation model now in place, the primary unmitigated risk in v0 is smart contract vulnerabilities. The contracts have not been audited. A formal audit is the critical pre-mainnet requirement. The deployer EOA can call pause() on LoanManager to halt new loan creation as an emergency stop, but cannot recover funds already in the protocol if a vulnerability is exploited before detection. The v0.5 upgrade to a multisig significantly reduces the risk of a single-key compromise.
